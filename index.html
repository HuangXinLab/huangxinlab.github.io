Research Summary

Dr. Xin Huang’s research focuses on developmental biology and its dysregulation in diseases such as leukemia and pediatric solid tumors, aiming to elucidate underlying mechanisms and develop new targeted therapeutic strategies.

By integrating multidisciplinary approaches including single-cell multi-omics, biophysics, cell and molecular biology, her work has identified and mechanistically validated novel tumor targets and combination therapies to overcome drug resistance, facilitating multiple national and international clinical translational studies.

As first or corresponding author, Dr. Huang has published in journals such as Cancer Cell, Developmental Cell, Nature Communications, JBC, and STAR Protocols. Her research has been covered by leading domestic and international media outlets, including ScienceDaily and BioArt.

The Huang Lab currently focuses on:

  • Leveraging integrated “wet” and “dry” approaches with clinical tumor cohorts to identify and functionally characterize drug targets related to developmental stage, clonal heterogeneity, and the tumor microenvironment, thereby providing new strategies for diagnosis and clinical translation of therapies.
  • Collaborating in frontier areas such as stem cell epigenetics, cell-fate determination, and translational research.
